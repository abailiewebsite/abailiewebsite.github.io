About Nokia

We once knew Nokia as an industry leader in cell phones through-out the early 2000s. Famous for their durability and battery life, these devices were recognized around the world. Since then, Nokia has sold its cell phone division and has transitioned to other forms of revenue streams. Now focusing more on business to business software, being a leader in 5G technology, cloud services, and the internet of things. With operations in 130 countries, Nokia is the first global communications solutions provider.


Learning Goals

In the first weeks of working at Nokia in each term, I outlined three clear goals that I deemed were applicable to my role at the time, along with some personal goals I had for myself. These goals consisted of both skills I wanted to learn and that I considered would be beneficial to my skillset as both a project manager or a developer.

Term #4 - Project Coordinator

Goal #1 - To be a proficent public speaker.

Goal Description

Speaking amongst large groups is a common task as a project manager. From planning sprints to daily stand-ups, being a confident speaker is an essential skill that I set out to hone.

Action Plan

Run team meetings and stand-ups.

Measure of Success

Team meetings are run efficiently without any hiccups.

Reflection

Running meets and presenting PowerPoints that I prepared was a daily occurrence. This gave me ample opportunity to practice and hone my public speaking skill. Over the 4 months, I increased my confidence with each meeting I had, and looking back now can confidently say I am a better public speaker.

Goal #2 - To be an effective project coordinator and an asset to the team.

Goal Description

I consistently strive to be an effective team member with each role I take on. With this being my first time in this role, I had lots to learn. From mastering JIRA on another level to being proficient with Excel, these were all tasks I had to strive at.

Action Plan

Stay on top of daily tasks, ask questions when needed.

Measure of Success

I excel in my daily activities and responsibilities.

Reflection

I quickly learned the tools and duties required of a project coordinator and was able to complete my work quickly and effectively.

Goal #3 - Stay on task and avoid distraction, use time efficently.

Goal Description

The project coordinator role is very scheduled and time-oriented. The role required a lot of meetings throughout the day which meant your work had to be completed between each meeting, leaving no time for distractions.

Action Plan

Keep myself busy with little time to mess around.

Measure of Success

Goals for each day/week are completed.

Reflection

The duties and requirements of the role itself made it hard to be distracted with such a tight schedule. This allowed me to stay on track with everything I had to do.

I have never been a part of a team that releases continuous updates for their product each month. The working dynamic in this team was very different due to this as everything had a hard due date. I wanted to prove to myself I could function effectively in a team like this.

Job Description

During my time at Nokia, I held two different positions over two different work terms. For the first time, I stepped out of the developer role and into a management role where I was working as a Project Coordinator. After the completion of that Winter term, and the pandemic just arriving, Nokia offered me a position for the summer as a Security Software Developer. In my Project Coordinator role, I looked over two divisions, the U.X. and Security division. I organized sprints and ran stand-ups for these divisions, as well as allocated work and tracked progress. With my transition to the developer role, I worked with the Security team and specifically on the CKEY component.

While working as a Project Coordinator, my typical day would start with one big meeting in the morning. This meeting was a big stand-up for the entire U.X. division. Compared to the Security division, the U.X. division wasn't that big so we were able to fit it all in one big meeting in the morning. This meeting was your regular stand-up, with everyone giving an update of that they were working on, I would call each person by name to give their update. This meeting had people in the room, as well as people tuning in over video chat from Finland, Portugal, and California. Later in the afternoon, I would host the stand-up for the Security division, the Security division was large and consisted of six components including CKEY, CSKM, CCFK, CCHT, CMAP, CRPT. In the meeting, I would call on everyone in each component to give their update. The majority of the Security team was located in California, which required me to set up the video call each meeting. In between meetings I would be preparing documentation, such as a triage of tickets for both divisions of what needed to be done and what was in a critical state. This was an email sent out each morning for both divisions. Other documentation I would prepare would be the end of sprint metrics, sprint kick-off slides and component support documentation.

In my role as a Security Software Developer, I primarily tended to support tickets from customers that came in through JIRA. I was responsible for any tickets relating to the CKEY component. The CKEY component was Nokia's version of the open-source software called Keycloak. Keycloak is used to allow single sign-on for web applications where you can manage users and generate key passes. Nokia takes this open-source software and builds on top of it, adding their own features and look. As a developer on this team, I would contribute to many feature additions and bug fixes to this software.

Both co-op's came with there challenges and learning curves, but now I can confidently add new skills to my resume that I can bring into the workforce after I graduate.
